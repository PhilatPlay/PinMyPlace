Problem Statement

Large portions of Ecuador's population live in areas without formal street addresses, particularly in:

  • Rural communities in Amazon and Andean regions
  • Informal settlements on the outskirts of Quito, Guayaquil, and Cuenca
  • Remote villages with traditional indigenous governance structures
  • New developments where streets haven't been officially registered
~40% Of Ecuador's population lacks formal street addresses
2M+ People unable to receive postal mail reliably
60% Of undelivered mail due to addressing issues

Impact on Citizens

Without reliable mail delivery, Ecuadorians in unaddressed areas face significant challenges:

  • ‚ùå Cannot receive government documents (IDs, tax forms, legal notices)
  • ‚ùå Excluded from e-commerce and online shopping
  • ‚ùå Cannot participate in mail-in voting or censuses
  • ‚ùå Miss important healthcare notifications
  • ‚ùå Unable to receive bank cards, contracts, or official correspondence

Previous Attempts at Solutions

Ecuador's postal service had tried several approaches, all with limited success:

  • General delivery/poste restante: Required travel to central post offices (impractical for rural areas)
  • Landmark-based addressing: Inconsistent and confusing ("near the big tree, past the blue house")
  • Community pickup points: Low adoption, security concerns with unattended packages
  • Written directions: Time-consuming for postal workers, high error rates

üí° The dropLogik Solution

Digital Addressing via GPS + QR Codes

dropLogik provides a simple, scalable solution that works even in the most remote areas:

How It Works for Ecuador

  1. Citizens create digital addresses - Visit local post office or use smartphone to pin their exact GPS location
  2. Postal service distributes trial codes - Free access codes provided during pilot program
  3. QR codes generated - Each household receives a unique QR code (printed or digital)
  4. Display or share QR code - Post on door, gate, or provide to postal service database
  5. Mail carriers scan and navigate - GPS directions lead directly to the location
